\begin{abstract}
We introduce the Fractal Tennis (FT), a purely geometric, recursively generated ontology governed by a foundational admissibility principle---the Elliott Stability Principle---which requires that every mathematical operation invoked as an axiom remains structurally stable under the theory's own evolution.
Smooth-manifold physics presupposes infinite divisibility and stable differentiable neighborhoods as primitive. In FT, space is instead an infinite recursive Apollonian sphere packing governed by discrete scale transformation ($\lambda$) and M\"obius inversion ($r \mapsto 1/r$). All dynamics emerge from these transformations together with conservation of angular momentum ($\mathbf{L}$).
We demonstrate that:
\begin{itemize}
\item The action kernel is geometric and proportional to $1/r$.
\item Its Taylor expansion yields a scale-invariant propagation speed $c$, the Newtonian $1/r^2$ interaction, and higher-order corrections.
\item The Apollonian volume series produces a geometric density ratio $\sim 0.85$ for the non-baryonic component.
\item Cosmological redshift arises from discrete scale traversal rather than metric expansion.
\end{itemize}
The framework contains no primitive smooth manifold, no PDE-based field ontology, and no metric expansion, while retaining effective correspondence with known large-scale physics.
\end{abstract}
