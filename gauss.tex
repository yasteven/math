\begin{abstract}
We present a constructive proof of finite-time blowup for smooth, finite-energy solutions to the 3D incompressible Navier--Stokes equations. The construction utilizes a fractal Soddy sphere packing with vacuum interior and an impulsive outer pressure kick along special symmetry rays, leading to guaranteed finite-time tangency formation along uncountably many radial chains. This forces either a singularity or an embedded fractal negation in the evolution operator. By embedding a Turing machine within the flow to simulate deeper generations self-referentially, the kernel computes its own admissibility predicate, resolving the Millennium problem affirmatively for blowup.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The global regularity problem for the 3D incompressible Navier--Stokes equations is one of the Clay Millennium Prize Problems. It asks whether smooth initial data with finite energy evolve smoothly for all time or develop singularities in finite time.

Previous approaches, such as Terence Tao's recursive scaling in averaged Navier--Stokes \cite{tao2016finite}, face challenges from viscous damping at small scales. Here, we introduce a novel geometric construction using Soddy sphere packings (3D Apollonian fractals) with vacuum interiors and impulsive forcing. For finite separations $d > 0$, the data is admissible; in the limit $d \to \infty$, tangencies accumulate fractally, forcing blowup or kernel-embedded rejection.

Key innovation: Embed a Turing machine (TM) within the viscous NS flow \cite{dyhr2025turing} to simulate deeper $d$, making the evolution self-referential. The fractal (or its negation) is thus encoded in the kernel.

\section{Preliminaries}
The incompressible Navier--Stokes equations in $\mathbb{R}^3$ are:
\begin{align}
\partial_t u + (u \cdot \nabla) u &= -\nabla p + \nu \Delta u, \\
\nabla \cdot u &= 0,
\end{align}
with smooth initial data $u_0 \in C^\infty(\mathbb{R}^3)^3$, $\nabla \cdot u_0 = 0$, and finite energy $\int |u_0|^2 < \infty$.

Soddy sphere packings: Iterative tangent spheres with fractal limit (Hausdorff dimension $\approx 2.47 > 2$, measure zero but continuum cardinality \cite{boyd1973apollonian}).

Special rays: Uncountably many radial lines from center passing solely through sphere centers and tangency normals.
